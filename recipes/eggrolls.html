Egg Rolls

Description

These egg rolls are crispy, seasoned, and the perfect side for your favorite Chinese foods! They can be filled with the protein of your choice, alongside cabbage and carrots, and can be served with duck sauce, or soy sauce, depending on your preference.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. ground chicken
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp. ginger, minced
  • 1/2 tsp. kosher salt
  • 1/4 tsp. ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp Chinese five spice powder
  • 1/2 cup dry shredded coleslaw mix
  • 1/2 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 1 tbsp. sesame oil
  • 12 egg roll wrappers
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • Neutral oil with a high smoke point (such as vegetable, canola, or peanut oil) for frying

Steps

  1. In a large skillet or wok set over medium heat, add the ground chicken, garlic, and ginger. Season with salt, pepper, and Chinese five spice powder. Cook until the chicken is browned and cooked through, breaking it up into small pieces as it cooks.

  2. Add in the shredded coleslaw, carrots, green onions, and sesame oil. Stir to combine and cook for 4-5 minutes until the vegetables are slightly softened.

  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the egg and 1 tablespoon of water.

  4. Lay an egg roll wrapper on a clean surface with one corner pointing towards you Spoon 2 table spoons of the chicken mixture onto the center of the wrapper. Fold the bottom corner up over the filling, then fold the sides in towards the center. Brush the top corner with the egg wash and roll the wrapper tightly to seal.

  5. Repeat with the remaining egg roll wrappers and filling.

  6. Heat 2 inches of oil in a large, deep skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat until it reaches 350 degrees fahrenheit. Fry the egg rolls in batches until they are golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes per side.

  7. Drain the egg rolls on a wire rack set over a baking sheet.

  8. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.

  9. This is the most important step: Enjoy!
